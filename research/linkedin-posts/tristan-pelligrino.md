I used to think the goal was to scale an agency like a SaaS company.

Grow fast. Add headcount. Chase the hockey stick.

Then I actually ran one. Solid it. Started a new one. And acquired a few more.

I now realize agencies aren't built for exponential scale. And that's not a flaw, it's the nature of the model.

At their heart, agencies are professional services organizations. Every new client requires more people. More coordination. More complexity. The math just doesn't work the way it does for software.

The best agencies I've seen grow 15-30% a year. That's the benchmark. That's what "high growth" looks like in this world.

And honestly? That's fine.

Because chasing scale for scale's sake is how you end up with bloated teams, razor-thin margins, and founders who are burned out and trapped.

Now, here's where it gets interesting.

The agencies that figure out AI (not as a gimmick, but as a real part of their workflow) are going to break the old rules. Not by adding more people, but by getting more leverage out of the people they have.

I think we're going to see a wave of super-niche agencies emerge. Smaller teams. Deep expertise. Premium positioning. Human judgment paired with AI-powered delivery.

These agencies will grow margins faster than the old model ever allowed. And big headcount? It won't be the signal of a high-growth agency anymore. It might actually be a warning sign.

I'm not talking about 10x growth overnight. 

I'm talking about margin expansion. EBITDA improvements that weren't possible five years ago. Doing more with a leaner, more focused team.

That's the shift I'm watching closely.

The agencies that win the next five years won't be the ones who scaled headcount the fastest. They'll be the ones who built systems around the best people.
