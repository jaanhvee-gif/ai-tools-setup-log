Over the last few years, I’ve realised my best ideas rarely happen at my desk - they often come in the gaps between everything else.

And this has been even clearer this month when I’ve been travelling more than usual and I keep noticing that those “in-between” moments - taxis, airport queues, short flights etc - are when my head feels clearest and ideas seem to flow more easily. But they’re also the exact moments when sitting down to type something out feels weirdly effortful.

And that’s where Wispr Flow has been genuinely helpful for my productivity. I’ve been using it to keep work moving even when I’m not at my desk, most directly with helping me talk out ideas and instructions to Claude Code and OpenClaw while I’ve been on the move.

So rather than waiting until I’m back at my laptop to properly articulate what I’m trying to do, I can capture the thought in the moment, get it into words, and keep building stuff literally wherever I am.

And Wispr Flow is voice-first writing across the apps you already use so it's not a chat assistant, and I’ve found it's been way easier to articulate my thoughts for what I want to build by speaking out my ideas rather than trying to spend time polishing a perfect prompt.

It’s also helped with the long-reply problem that I'm often facing for messages and stuff. When someone sends a paragraph and you want to respond in detail, that extra bit of effort can be enough to push it onto the “I’ll do it later” pile. But being able to speak the first draft of a reply (and then quickly edit it before sending) has made it so much easier for me to get back to people properly and quickly.

Anyway, Wispr Flow has been genuinely needle moving for my productivity, especially as I’ve been building new systems into our business over the last few weeks. It’s available on Mac, Windows, iPhone, and Android and you can check it out here - https://lnkd.in/gr2NaMHV.
